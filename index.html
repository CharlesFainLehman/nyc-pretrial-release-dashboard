Released and Rearrested

Pretrial Release Outcomes in New York City, 2020–2025

New York's 2020 bail reform sharply limited judges' ability to set bail or detain defendants before trial. The law bars judges from considering whether a defendant poses a danger to the public—making New York the only state with such a restriction.

The data below, drawn from the state's own DCJS Supplemental Pretrial Release Data File, show what has followed: hundreds of thousands of defendants released at arraignment, and tens of thousands rearrested before their cases were resolved.

How Are Defendants Released?

Under bail reform, the vast majority of defendants are released at arraignment—most on their own recognizance (ROR), without any conditions. Bail is set in a small fraction of cases, and outright remand is rare.

What Happens After Release?

Among defendants whose cases have concluded, roughly one in five was rearrested before disposition—including thousands charged with felonies and violent crimes.
